Tribute to A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, was an eminent Indian scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Kalam graduated in aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology. He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

One of Kalam's notable contributions was his work on the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which bolstered India's defense capabilities. He was also instrumental in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which made India a nuclear-armed state.
